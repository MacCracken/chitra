# 0003 — Consumer ABI: 16-byte GpuErr-compatible ChitraErr + append-only ChitraImage

**Status**: Accepted
**Date**: 2026-06-26

## Context

chitra ships as a single strip-include dist bundle — `dist/chitra.cyr`,
regenerated by `cyrius distlib` (see
[../architecture/002-flat-modules-distlib-concatenation.md](../architecture/002-flat-modules-distlib-concatenation.md)).
A consumer (mabda's `gpu_texture_load_png`) does not link against an
opaque library with a stable, name-mangled symbol ABI — it folds the
concatenated source in and calls the decode functions directly.

Cyrius has no opaque-handle facility and no stable cross-bundle symbol
ABI. Records are plain byte buffers; field access is `load64`/`store64`
at a constant byte offset (`src/png.cyr:32-38` declares the offsets as
`IMAGE_*_OFFSET` constants). When mabda reads a decoded image it reads
those same offsets, and when a decode fails it maps the returned
`ChitraErr` onto its own `GPU_ERR_IMAGE_DECODE`. There is no translation
layer to hide a layout change behind.

So the in-memory record layout **is** the ABI. Two records cross the
consumer boundary:

- **`ChitraErr`** — `src/error.cyr:9-11`: a 16-byte record, `+0` code,
  `+8` detail pointer. Returned by-pointer through the `err_out` slot of
  `chitra_png_decode` (`src/png.cyr:63`).
- **`ChitraImage`** — `src/png.cyr:20-38`: the decode result. As of
  0.2.1 it is 48 bytes: `width@0`, `height@8`, `pixels@16` (owned RGBA8),
  `channels@24` (always 4), `seen_iend@32`, `src_ctype@40`.

A decision was needed about how these layouts may evolve, because a
silent reorder or width-repurpose would corrupt mabda's reads at runtime
with no compile-time signal.

## Decision

**Treat the byte-offset layout of the consumer-facing records as a public
contract, with two specific rules.**

(a) **`ChitraErr` is a fixed 16-byte record deliberately
layout-compatible with mabda's `GpuErr`** — `+0` code, `+8` detail
pointer (`src/error.cyr:9-11`, `chitra_err_new` at `:39-44`). The shared
shape means a chitra decode failure maps onto `GPU_ERR_IMAGE_DECODE`
with zero translation: mabda reads the code and detail pointer at the
offsets its own `GpuErr` already uses.

(b) **`ChitraImage` fields are append-only.** The 0.1.x quartet —
`width@0`, `height@8`, `pixels@16`, `channels@24` — keeps those offsets
forever. 0.2.0 added `seen_iend@32` and `src_ctype@40` strictly at the
end and bumped `CHITRA_IMAGE_SIZE` from 32 to 48 (`src/png.cyr:38`);
existing accessors and mabda's reads were untouched. Any future field
appends at the next free offset and bumps the size constant. **No field
ever reorders, shrinks, or is repurposed within a major version.**

In scope: the two records above and their accessor functions. Out of
scope: internal records (e.g. the parse-raw intermediate), which carry no
consumer contract and may change freely.

## Consequences

**Positive**:
- Consumers never break on a minor/patch bump. mabda compiled against
  0.1.x reads a 0.2.1 `ChitraImage` correctly because every offset it
  knows about is unchanged.
- Error mapping is free — the `GpuErr`-compatible 16-byte shape means no
  per-call translation struct on the consumer side.
- Additive widening is safe: a new trailing field is invisible to a
  consumer that does not read it.

**Negative**:
- A slot can never be shrunk, reordered, or repurposed. Every offset is
  spent permanently for the life of the major version.
- The layout is a public contract even pre-1.0, narrowing what a minor
  bump may touch more than SemVer alone would imply for an ordinary
  internal struct.

**Neutral**:
- A genuine breaking layout change (reorder, shrink, repurpose) is still
  possible — it just costs a major version bump, a `Breaking` entry in
  [../../CHANGELOG.md](../../CHANGELOG.md), and a new ADR superseding this
  one. The rule constrains minors, not the project forever.

## Alternatives considered

- **Opaque handle + accessor-only ABI** (consumers hold a token, never
  touch offsets). Rejected: Cyrius offers no stable symbol/handle ABI
  across the dist concatenation — the bundle is folded in as source, so
  there is nothing to hide the layout behind. Byte-offset access is the
  language idiom here (see
  [../architecture/002-flat-modules-distlib-concatenation.md](../architecture/002-flat-modules-distlib-concatenation.md)).
  Append-only offsets give most of the stability benefit an opaque handle
  would, at no abstraction cost.
- **A tagged-union `Result` instead of the `(ptr, err_out)` pair.**
  Rejected: the `(ptr, 0-on-fail, *err_out set)` shape matches the
  established `chitra_png_parse_raw` convention (`src/png.cyr:63-83`) and
  maps directly onto mabda's `GpuErr` flow. A tagged union would add a
  carrier record with its own layout contract and buy nothing the pair
  does not already give.
